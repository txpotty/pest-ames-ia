Ant Infestations

Ants are one of the most frequent complaints we receive. These persistent insects can invade homes and businesses in large numbers, seeking food and shelter. While most ants are just a nuisance, some species can contaminate food or cause structural damage. Effective ant control involves identifying the species, locating the colony, and using targeted treatments to eliminate the entire population and prevent future invasions. DIY methods often only address the visible ants, leaving the colony intact and ready to rebound. Our professional ant control services tackle the problem at its source.

Termite Damage

Termites are wood-destroying insects that can cause significant structural damage to your property before you even realize they are there. These silent invaders feed on cellulose found in wood, silently weakening beams, floors, and walls. Signs of termites can include discarded wings near windowsills, mud tubes on foundations, or hollow-sounding wood. Early detection and professional termite treatment are crucial to prevent costly repairs. Our comprehensive termite services include inspections, targeted treatments, and preventative measures to protect your investment.

Bed Bug Problems

Bed bugs are tiny, elusive pests that feed on human blood, causing itchy bites and significant discomfort. These hitchhikers can easily be brought into homes through luggage, used furniture, or clothing. Once established, a bed bug infestation can be incredibly difficult to eradicate without professional help. They hide in cracks and crevices, particularly around beds, and reproduce quickly. Bed bug extermination requires a thorough and multi-faceted approach, often involving heat treatments and targeted insecticides. We provide discrete and effective bed bug solutions to restore your peace of mind.

Silverfish Issues

Silverfish are nocturnal insects that feed on starches and sugars, including paper, glue, clothing, and stored foods. Their presence can indicate moisture problems in your home. While not harmful to humans, they can cause damage to books, fabrics, and stored items. Silverfish control involves reducing moisture levels, sealing entry points, and applying targeted treatments to areas where they hide and feed. Discovering silverfish in your home can be unsettling, and while they aren't a direct threat to your health, they can cause damage to your belongings. These ancient-looking insects thrive in damp, humid environments and feed on a variety of materials containing carbohydrates, such as paper, books, photographs, textiles, and even wallpaper glue. Their feeding can leave behind irregular holes, etching, or yellow stains. The presence of silverfish often indicates underlying moisture issues within your home, such as leaky pipes, poor ventilation, or high humidity. Addressing these moisture problems is a critical part of effective silverfish control. Our professional services involve a thorough inspection to identify areas of high moisture and locate where silverfish are hiding and feeding. We then apply targeted treatments to eliminate the current infestation, focusing on humid areas like bathrooms, kitchens, basements, and attics. Moth Infestations

Certain types of moths can cause damage to clothing, carpets, and stored food products. Clothing moths feed on natural fibers, leaving holes in garments, while pantry moths infest dry goods like grains, cereals, and nuts. Identifying the type of moth and locating the source of the infestation are key to effective moth control. This often involves removing infested items, cleaning affected areas thoroughly, and applying targeted treatments or using pheromone traps to capture adult moths and break the breeding cycle. Protecting your valuable textiles and keeping your pantry free of pests requires a systematic approach. While many moths are harmless and even beautiful, certain species can be a significant nuisance and cause damage within your home. Clothes moths and pantry moths are the most common culprits for indoor infestations. Clothes moths feed on natural fibers found in clothing, carpets, upholstery, and even taxidermy, leaving behind irregular holes and tell-tale silk tubes or webbing. Pantry moths infest dry food items like grains, cereals, birdseed, and pet food, contaminating them with their larvae, webbing, and droppings. Discovering moths flying around your home, especially in kitchen or closet areas, is a strong indicator of an infestation. Effective moth control requires identifying the specific type of moth and locating the source of the infestation. For clothes moths, this means thoroughly inspecting closets, drawers, and storage areas for larvae and damage. For pantry moths, it involves checking all susceptible food items. Our professional moth control services involve identifying the moth species and implementing a tailored treatment plan. For clothes moths, this may include targeted insecticide applications to infested areas and advice on proper storage of susceptible items. For pantry moths, it involves the careful removal of all infested food products, thorough cleaning of pantry shelves, and the use of pheromone traps to capture adult males and monitor for ongoing activity. We also provide guidance on preventative measures, such as storing susceptible items in airtight containers and rotating stored foods regularly. Don't let moths ruin your clothing or contaminate your food; rely on our expertise for effective moth control solutions in Story City.
